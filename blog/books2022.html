Blog 1 - Book Recommendations in 2023

As we enter the new year, many of us are looking for ways to improve ourselves and expand our knowledge. One great way to do this is through reading, and there are so many amazing books out there to choose from. Here are a few recommendations for books to add to your reading list in 2023:

1. "Educated" by Tara Westover

Tara Westover's memoir "Educated" tells the story of her journey from growing up in a strict, abusive household in rural Idaho to earning a PhD from Cambridge University. Along the way, Westover confronts and eventually breaks free from the harmful beliefs and behaviors that have held her back. "Educated" is a powerful and inspiring read that will leave you thinking long after you've finished it.

2. "The Nickel Boys" by Colson Whitehead

In "The Nickel Boys," Colson Whitehead tells the fictionalized story of Elwood Curtis, a young black man who is sent to a juvenile detention center in the Jim Crow South. The Nickel Academy, as the center is called, is supposed to be a place of rehabilitation, but in reality it is a place of brutality and abuse. Based on the real-life Dozier School for Boys, "The Nickel Boys" is a heart-wrenching and eye-opening look at the history of racial injustice in the United States.

3. "Normal People" by Sally Rooney

"Normal People" follows the complicated relationship between Connell and Marianne, two Irish high school students who come from very different backgrounds. As they navigate their way through college and beyond, they struggle to understand and connect with each other, and with themselves. Sally Rooney's writing is raw, honest, and deeply affecting, making "Normal People" a must-read for anyone looking for a poignant and relatable love story.

4. "The Overstory" by Richard Powers

In "The Overstory," Richard Powers tells the interconnected stories of nine different characters whose lives are all touched by trees in some way. From a scientist studying the intelligence of plants to a group of activists trying to save a redwood forest, each character's story is unique and compelling. "The Overstory" is a sweeping, ambitious novel that will make you see the natural world in a whole new way.

5. "Daisy Jones and The Six" by Taylor Jenkins Reid

"Daisy Jones and The Six" is a fictionalized account of a real-life 70s rock band and their tumultuous rise to fame. Told through a series of interviews with the band members and those who knew them, the novel explores the blurred lines between fact and fiction, and the enduring power of music. If you're a fan of rock and roll or just a good old-fashioned rags-to-riches story, "Daisy Jones and The Six" is a must-read.

These are just a few of the many great books that are worth checking out in 2023. Whatever your interests, there's sure to be a book out there that speaks to you. Happy reading!
